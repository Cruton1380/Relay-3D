### The Commission Flow Pathway

When economic activity occurs on Relay (such as a donation to a channel or a paid service), funds flow through a predetermined pathway with commission splits determined by community vote:

1. **Global Commission (0.1-0.5%)**: A small percentage goes to the global treasury, funding core infrastructure, security, and development that benefits all regions. This rate is determined by global community voting.

2. **Regional Commission (0.2-1%)**: The next layer goes to the regional treasury of the geographic region where the transaction originated. This funds regional priorities like local infrastructure, emergency response, and community programs.

3. **Channel Commission (0-5%)**: For transactions within specific channels, a commission is distributed among elected channel positions (like developers, moderators, or specialized roles the channel requires).

4. **Remaining Funds**: After commissions are distributed, the remaining funds stay in the channel or go to the specified recipient.

**A practical example**: When Alice donates 100,000 sats to her local transit solutions channel:
- 200 sats (0.2%) might go to the global treasury
- 300 sats (0.3%) might go to her California regional treasury
- 500 sats (0.5%) might be distributed among the channel's elected positions
- The remaining 99,000 sats (99%) stay in the channel to fund transit solutions

**Think of it this way**: Imagine if citizens could vote directly on tax rates and budget allocations in their city, with decisions automatically implemented when consensus is reached. That's how Relay's parameter voting works for all economic decisions.

### Stabilization and Implementation

To prevent rapid fluctuations in economic structures that could destabilize communities, Relay implements a stabilization period for economic decisions:

1. **Voting Period**: Community members cast votes on proposed commission structures
2. **Ranking Period**: Options are continuously ranked based on vote totals
3. **Stabilization Requirement**: Top-ranked option must maintain its position for a community-determined period (e.g., 4 weeks)
4. **Implementation**: Once stabilization is achieved, the winning structure is automatically implemented

**Real-world impact**: This deliberative process prevents economic "flash crashes" or manipulation while ensuring that parameters truly reflect sustained community consensus rather than momentary majority opinion.

For the "SF Transit Solutions" channel, Option A (Dev60/Comm25/Ops15) has been the community's top choice for 3 weeks, with 1 week remaining before implementation. This structure allocates:

- 60% of channel commission to the Developer Lead position
- 25% to the Community Manager position
- 15% to the Operations position

**Human-centered design**: The voting system was specifically designed to be accessible to non-technical users while preventing common manipulation tactics:

- Clear, visual representation of options
- Real-time feedback on voting status
- Transparency on participation levels
- Sybil-resistant voting through the Relay identity system
- Protection against last-minute voting swings

## ğŸ’° Commission Flow Mechanics

### Distribution Algorithm

When economic activity occurs on Relay, the commission distribution happens automatically through a deterministic algorithm that ensures accurate, transparent fund allocation:

```
Commission Distribution Algorithm:

1. Calculate Global Commission:
   global_amount = transaction_total * global_rate
   Subtract global_amount from transaction_total
   
2. Calculate Regional Commission:
   regional_amount = transaction_total * regional_rate
   Subtract regional_amount from transaction_total
   
3. Calculate Channel Commission:
   channel_amount = transaction_total * channel_rate
   Subtract channel_amount from transaction_total
   
4. Distribute Channel Commission:
   For each position in channel_positions:
     position_amount = channel_amount * position_percentage
     Allocate position_amount to position_holder
     
5. Remaining Funds:
   Allocate transaction_total to final_recipient
```

**Cryptographic verification**: Each step of this process generates zero-knowledge proofs that verify the calculations were performed correctly without revealing potentially sensitive transaction amounts.

### Worked Example: Donation Flow

Let's follow a real-world example of how funds flow through the system when a community member makes a donation:

**Scenario**: Alice donates 100,000 sats to the "SF Transit Solutions" channel

**Current Parameters** (all democratically determined through community voting):
- Global Commission Rate: 0.2%
- California Regional Commission Rate: 0.3%
- SF Transit Solutions Channel Commission Rate: 0.5%
- Channel Position Allocation: Dev Lead (60%), Community Manager (25%), Operations (15%)

**Step-by-Step Fund Flow**:

1. **Global Commission Calculation**:
   ```
   global_amount = 100,000 * 0.002 = 200 sats
   remaining = 100,000 - 200 = 99,800 sats
   ```
   *The 200 sats are transferred to the Global Treasury*

2. **Regional Commission Calculation**:
   ```
   regional_amount = 99,800 * 0.003 = 299.4 sats (rounded to 299 sats)
   remaining = 99,800 - 299 = 99,501 sats
   ```
   *The 299 sats are transferred to the California Regional Treasury*

3. **Channel Commission Calculation**:
   ```
   channel_amount = 99,501 * 0.005 = 497.5 sats (rounded to 498 sats)
   remaining = 99,501 - 498 = 99,003 sats
   ```
   *The 498 sats are distributed among channel positions:*
   
   ```
   Developer Lead: 498 * 0.6 = 298.8 sats (rounded to 299 sats)
   Community Manager: 498 * 0.25 = 124.5 sats (rounded to 125 sats)
   Operations: 498 * 0.15 = 74.7 sats (rounded to 74 sats)
   ```

4. **Final Allocation**:
   ```
   The remaining 99,003 sats stay in the SF Transit Solutions channel fund
   ```

**Transparency and Privacy**: While these calculations are completely transparent and verified on the blockchain, Alice's individual donation amount remains private. The system only shows that a donation was made and the commission splits were calculated correctly.

### Economic Impact Analysis

The commission structure creates several important economic effects that shape community behavior and system sustainability:

**1. Resource Alignment with Value Creation**:
Communities that generate more economic activity receive more resources through their regional treasury, creating a virtuous cycle of development and participation.

**2. Incentive for Quality Governance**:
Regions with effective treasury management attract more participation, increasing their economic activity and treasury size.

**3. Democratic Economic Control**:
Because all commission rates are subject to community voting, the system naturally finds equilibrium points that balance different needs:
- Too high global/regional rates reduce channel incentives
- Too low rates might underfund critical infrastructure
- Community voting discovers the optimal balance

**4. Progressive Economic Development**:
As regions develop stronger economic activity, they gain more resources to invest in further improvements, creating sustainable growth trajectories.

### Multi-Signature Security Architecture

Treasury management requires sophisticated signing systems to balance security, operational efficiency, and democratic control.

**Technical Implementation**:

```yaml
Multi-Signature Implementation:
  Key Architecture:
    # Threshold Signature Scheme (TSS): t-of-n approval without key reassembly
    # Distributed key generation prevents any party from having full key
    # Post-quantum cryptographic foundations for future security
    # Hardware-secured key shards for physical protection
  
  Authorization Flow:
    # Proposal created with unique identifier and execution payload
    # Signing quorum determined by amount and category thresholds
    # Required signers notified through secure channels
    # Each approval generates partial signature
    # Combined signature threshold triggers execution
  
  Security Features:
    # Time-bounded approval windows prevent delayed attacks
    # Contextual verification ensures signers understand what they're approving
    # Operation-specific keys prevent cross-operation replay
    # Circuit breakers for abnormal signing patterns
  
  Recovery Mechanisms:
    # Social recovery for lost signer keys
    # Progressive backup system for regional continuity
    # Constitutional override for emergency situations
    # Key rotation schedules for regular security updates
```

**Advanced Security: Shamir's Secret Sharing**:

The multi-signature system employs Shamir's Secret Sharing algorithm, which splits keys into multiple shards such that:
- Any combination of t+1 shards can reconstruct the key
- Any combination of t or fewer shards reveals nothing about the key
- No central party ever holds the complete key

This mathematical approach ensures that treasury access remains secure even if some key holders are compromised, while still enabling efficient operations when legitimate approvals are gathered.

### Scenario 1: Regional Investment Decision

**Meet Maria, the California Regional Governor**

Maria was elected as the California Regional Governor three months ago on a platform of improving rural connectivity. The region has accumulated $2.3M in its treasury from commission flows, and Maria wants to fulfill her campaign promise to expand mesh network coverage to underserved agricultural communities.

**Day 1: Proposal Development**

Maria works with her tech team to develop a comprehensive proposal:
- $450,000 investment in mesh network infrastructure
- Targeting 5 rural communities with poor connectivity
- Implementation timeline of 6 months
- Expected impact: 2,000+ residents gaining reliable access

She drafts the formal proposal in the Treasury Management interface, categorizing it as "Infrastructure Development" (which has a $920K quarterly allocation).

**Day 2: Official Review**

Because the expenditure exceeds $100K, it requires approval from 4 of the 5 regional officials:
1. Maria (Governor) reviews the final proposal and adds her signature
2. Alex (Tech Lead) analyzes the technical specifications and approves
3. Dana (Community Liaison) confirms the communities have been consulted and adds her signature
4. Robert (Treasury Manager) verifies the budget availability and signs
5. Lisa (Security Coordinator) is traveling and unavailable to sign

With 4 of 5 signatures collected, the proposal meets the required threshold.

**Day 3-7: Community Visibility Period**

Before execution, all major treasury allocations enter a mandatory 5-day visibility period:
- The full proposal is published to the California region's treasury dashboard
- Community members can view detailed plans, budgets, and expected outcomes
- Comments and questions are submitted by interested residents
- Maria and her team respond to feedback and make minor adjustments

**Day 8: Implementation**

With the visibility period complete and no significant concerns raised:
1. The treasury system automatically executes the fund allocation
2. $450,000 is transferred to the Infrastructure Development multisig wallet
3. Smart contracts establish milestone-based release schedules
4. The project begins with vendor selection and equipment procurement

**Months 1-6: Transparent Progress Tracking**

As implementation proceeds:
- Weekly progress updates are posted to the treasury dashboard
- Milestone completions trigger phased fund releases
- Community members in the target regions provide on-the-ground feedback
- Real-time coverage maps show expansion of the network
- Spending versus budget is continuously monitored

**Long-term Impact**:
After successful implementation, the project demonstrates concrete results:
- 2,300 previously underserved residents gain reliable connectivity
- Regional participation in Relay increases by 18% in the target areas
- Economic activity in the connected communities grows by 12%
- The success becomes a model for similar initiatives in other regions

### Scenario 2: Channel Position Economics

**Meet Alex, a Developer Contributing to Community Channels**

Alex is a software developer who actively contributes to several channels in the California region, particularly focusing on transit and urban planning tools. He's interested in taking on more formal responsibilities in the "SF Transit Solutions" channel, which currently has 5,000 active members and receives approximately 500,000 sats in monthly donations.

**Current Channel Context**:
- Channel Commission Rate: 0.5% (voted by channel members)
- Channel Position Structure: Dev Lead (60%), Community Manager (25%), Operations (15%)
- Monthly Channel Commission: ~2,500 sats (0.5% of 500,000)

**Week 1: Understanding the Economics**

Alex reviews the channel's economic dashboard to understand the potential compensation:
- Dev Lead position currently receives ~1,500 sats monthly (60% of 2,500)
- Position responsibilities include maintaining channel tools, developing new features, and technical support
- Current position holder has announced stepping down next month
- Next election is scheduled in 3 weeks

**Week 2: Election Campaign**

Alex decides to run for the Dev Lead position:
1. He submits his candidacy through the channel governance system
2. He publishes his development roadmap, focusing on new transit visualization tools
3. He participates in community Q&A sessions about his technical vision
4. He engages with current contributors to understand ongoing projects

**Week 3: Election Results**

The election results come in:
- Alex: 2,300 votes (51%)
- Other candidate: 2,200 votes (49%)
- Alex is elected as the new Dev Lead with a 12-month term

**Months 1-12: Position Economics**

As Dev Lead, Alex experiences the economic aspects of the position:
1. Monthly he receives his commission share automatically (1,500-2,000 sats depending on donations)
2. As he implements popular new features, channel membership grows to 7,000
3. Monthly donations increase to ~700,000 sats
4. His monthly commission increases proportionally to ~2,100 sats
5. His work building valuable tools increases both the channel's utility and its economic sustainability

**When Parameters Change**:

In month 7, channel members propose and vote on a new commission structure:
- New structure: Dev Lead (50%), Community Manager (25%), Operations (15%), Design (10%)
- After the 4-week stabilization period, the new structure is implemented
- Alex's share adjusts to 50% of channel commission
- Despite the percentage decrease, overall channel growth means his actual compensation remains similar

**Long-term Impact**:
Alex's contributions create a virtuous cycle:
- Better transit tools attract more community members
- Increased membership leads to more donations
- Higher donation volume means more resources for development
- More resources enable better tools and features
- The channel becomes the primary digital hub for transit planning in the city

Lisa (Security Coordinator) activates the treasury's emergency response protocol:

- $75,000 for immediate equipment replacement

3. Robert (Treasury Manager) completes the required signature set

### Scenario 4: Commission Rate Adjustment

**Meet Sarah, a Community Organizer Concerned About Infrastructure**

Sarah is an active member of the California region who believes the current regional commission rate (0.3%) is insufficient to fund necessary infrastructure improvements. She decides to propose increasing the regional rate to 0.5% to generate additional treasury resources.

**Week 1: Proposal Research and Development**

Sarah researches the economic impact of her proposed change:
- Current monthly regional commission: ~$180K (0.3% of regional activity)
- Projected monthly commission at 0.5%: ~$300K
- Additional $120K monthly for infrastructure projects

She develops a detailed proposal including:
- Current treasury constraints and unmet needs
- Specific projects that additional funding would enable
- Economic impact analysis for different community segments
- Alternative funding approaches considered

**Week 2: Community Discussion**

Sarah submits her proposal to the regional parameter governance system:
1. The system creates a structured discussion thread for community input
2. Regional officials provide perspective on treasury needs
3. Community members debate the pros and cons of increased commission rates
4. Economic models are shared showing different scenarios

**Week 3: Formal Voting Period**

After sufficient discussion, the proposal moves to formal voting:
- Option A: Increase regional commission to 0.5% (Sarah's proposal)
- Option B: Keep current 0.3% rate
- Option C: Smaller increase to 0.4% (compromise proposal)

The voting results after one week:
- Option A: 35% support
- Option B: 40% support
- Option C: 25% support

**Weeks 4-7: Stabilization Period**

With Option B (status quo) in the lead, the stabilization countdown begins:
- Option B must remain in first place for 4 weeks to be confirmed
- Community discussion continues during this period
- Additional information about infrastructure needs shifts some opinions
- By week 6, voting has changed:
  - Option A: 30% support
  - Option B: 32% support
  - Option C: 38% support

With Option C now in the lead, a new 4-week stabilization period begins.

**Week 11: Implementation**

After Option C maintains its lead for 4 weeks:
1. The system schedules the parameter change
2. Community members receive notification of the upcoming adjustment
3. On the implementation date, the regional commission automatically increases to 0.4%
4. Treasury projections are updated to reflect the ~$60K monthly increase

**Long-term Impact**:
The democratic parameter adjustment process results in:
- A compromise solution that balances different community perspectives
- Increased but moderate funding for regional infrastructure
- Community buy-in through transparent deliberation
- Empirical data on the effects of commission changes that will inform future discussions
