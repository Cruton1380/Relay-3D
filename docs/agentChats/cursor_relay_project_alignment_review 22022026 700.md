### Contract #318 — Video Conferencing & Camera Awareness Protocol (§39.7)

Video calling in Relay IS the detection pipeline. When users join a video call, their SCV begins passively training on face, hands, posture, voice, and environment — all processed locally, never transmitted. Five mandatory camera state indicators (OFF / CALL ACTIVE green / DETECTION ACTIVE amber / MESH CONTRIBUTING amber+blue / RECORDING red+label) ensure the user always knows when the camera is active. Hardware LED is always accurate — no silent camera mode exists. Hidden recording produces a procedural violation scar.

The key design insight: most users join Relay for normal video calls and don't realize on day one that filming enables voice commands, gesture shortcuts, and the full detection pipeline. By the time they discover spell casting, their SCV has already been learning their patterns for weeks. Detection levels escalate naturally with engagement: VOICE_ONLY → VOICE_GESTURE → FULL_DETECTION, following the fog reveal model.

### Contract #319 — Kinetic Input Rate (KIR) — Movement as Power Generation (§43.10.14)

Movement is a fixed, measurable combat input: `KIR(t) = Σ(velocity × mass × continuity)`. Stillness generates zero Action Points — a guard stance produces nothing. Sustained motion accumulates AP for spell amplification, combo extension, defensive recovery, and crowd engagement. The continuity multiplier rewards unbroken motion and resets on pause.

This is why nunchucks spinning continuously generate more AP than a sword held in stance. Three strategic styles emerge: aggressive (high KIR, high AP, more exposure), defensive (zero KIR, safe but no AP), rhythmic (sustained moderate KIR, balanced). All conversion rates are Category A parameters.

### Contract #320 — Physical Sport Arenas — Paintball as Battle Training (§68.20)

Any real-world sport in a camera-equipped space becomes a Relay arena with live detection, real-time scoring, AR destruction overlays, and full SCV tracking. Paintball is the reference implementation: cameras detect paint splatter on players and walls, track projectile trajectories, and map player movement. AR overlays render hits as destruction graphics that accumulate over the match — the battlefield visually transforms from pristine to war-torn.

Markers become spell weapons, paint colors become elements, firing is spell casting with Power cost. SCV tracks shot accuracy, body-zone precision scoring, KIR, territory control, suppressive fire effectiveness. The model generalizes to laser tag, archery, fencing, martial arts, and dodgeball.

### Contract #321 — Founder Presentation Format — User Sphere Reveal (§88.10)
